What makes Denver, Denver?

The culture of a place is defined largely by the actions of those who inhabit it.  This blog seeks to explore the ways in which the people of Denver interact with their surroundings on a daily basis as well as the creative and unexpected ways in which they have re-purposed elements of both the natural and built environments to fit their needs.  As designers, it is critical to consider the identity of a city and the urban interventions that contribute to it at a multitude of scales in order to help define the way forward.

Tanner Hyland

// DENVER, CO // 2017.09.05

The task of accommodating even higher levels of density in major U.S. cities seems daunting to say the least, but the emergence of integrated technologies such as those produced by Sidewalk Labs has provided architects with a new set of tools in the quest for sustainable futures.  With plans in the making to develop a “Futuristic Mini-City” in Toronto, the company has ventured to imagine what a city might look like if built “from the internet up.”

 

At the core of Sidewalk Labs’ operations is the concept of urban connectivity: the seamless integration of a variety of systems to provide real-time data that allows cities to adapt and develop according to the people who inhabit them.  Embracing such technological advances is essential for architects to maximize the capacity of urban environments, and understanding the ways in which our profession can collaborate with leaders in the tech industry represents a major step in doing so.

// Denver, CO // 2017.09.12

14th St. Bikeway

With an increasing number of people biking to work on a daily basis, Denver’s investment in infrastructure to support this trend has materialized in the form of the new 14th Street Bikeway.  Previously relying on pavement markings to delineate the space reserved for cyclists on Denver’s crowded downtown streets, the 14th Street Bikeway is the city’s first curbed bike lane, creating the impetus for more people to join the movement emphasizing alternative transportation.
