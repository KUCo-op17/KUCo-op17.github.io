Art Alley

“I hid a, hid a chair over there in that corner there yesterday, someone must’ve stole it…”

-Local homeless man (inaudible)

 

1

Located in a very old and worn down area, extremely poor and patchy surface conditions in the alley, with many weeds and overgrown areas, littered with trash, but still such a beautiful part of the city. The alley is very skinny and is the back door route for many businesses such as a distillery, an art studio, a gym, and others. Almost all the surfaces of the buildings facing this alley are completely covered with streets art.

We believe that this was never intended to be a popular publicly-known location. Due to its hidden nature, alleys such as this are prime locations for graffiti writers and street artists to have free uninterrupted expression. Over time the underground art community recognized this alley for its variety of content and style, and so more and more attention was brought to it, bringing more, artists, then more art, and all of the sudden you have a unique source of culture.

Public Bike Stations

Near a bus stop on the west side of Main Street on the outer edge of the Old Westport district. The surrounding environment consists of large mass buildings spanning full blocks with multiple storefront businesses within them. Main street is very wide in this part of the city and it’s sidewalks are very wide as well. It is a 3 way intersection with Westport Road ending into main.

We believe this to be located here because it is a very commercial area with many places to shop and eat. Westport has a very large amount of foot traffic and this station serves to connect this major part of the city with the other parts via these bike stations.

The Metal Man

Located in an old area with moderate traffic on the North-East corner of the intersection, This guy has been here for a very long time. There is the grinders event space one block south, with Grinders Pizza and bar down the street. On the South- East corner of the intersection is the International Tap House, and on the north side of the street is the Border Brewing Company.

We believe this was placed here by a local artist that knows the area and people well. This strip of 18th street sees a large amount of vehicle and foot traffic, and the area is well known for the Grinders outdoor event space. This metal man is an expression of the rough yet artistic vibe of the area.
