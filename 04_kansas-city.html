What makes KC, KC?

As we begin to pull back the layers and scratch the surface of what gives a city it's character. The world is a complicated system; full of fleeting moments and timeless monuments. By merely walking outside we are an audience to the grand decisions others have made to impact our world — finding parking, if we bike to work, where our parks are, and even how we figure out where to go for lunch.

Oppenstein Brothers Memorial Park

"Ever since I moved into teh condos there, I'e come here every day to take my dog out." - Anonymous

Located just south of the of the Wallstreet Tower condominiums, this park sits in a very dense part of the city surrounded by high rise buildings. The park contains no grass and is covered in a variety of brick and stone. There are many planters containing many different grasses, bushes, and flowers. There are a few trees scattered along the outer edges of the park providing shade to many different benches and ledges and tables while leaving the middle completely open to the sun. In the middle of the park lies a large interactive turn table, called the Star Disk, which depicts the exact stars and constellations that are overhead when the current date and time is put in.

We think this intervention is located here primarily as a quiet resting place for people walk their dogs, have lunch, read a book in the shade, etc. With the condos adjacent to it, it is frequented by those living there. We also believe it to be a form of educational artwork that attracts people and instantly becomes a destination.

The Bike Box

“I take this bus every day but I don’t see people riding their bikes around here.” - Anonymous

Located at a 3-way intersection on the north-south crosswalk on the west side of the street. There is a bus stop on the South-West corner of the intersection. There is a bridge overhead connecting the two buildings on the Noth and South side of 11th street. the bike box is a painted “bikes only” zone in the road that allows cyclists to pull in front of vehicles so they can see around the corners without obstruction.

This intervention was located here because it is a very busy and dangerous intersection with skinny lanes and limited views. Between the city bus, the streetcar and all the traffic Main street receives, this box appears vital for biker safty and overall traffic communication and flow.

18 Broadway Urban Garden

Located in the crossroads district, a block south of the Kauffman Performing Arts Center, there is a parking lot and small bsinesses across the street to the West and small businesses/private property to the South and East. The garden contains a vast variety of plants and trees that are all specifically located, along with the design of the topography, to collect and filter rain water. The surrounding environment has no more than three story buildings with wide sidewalks along the street. All he buildings are somewhat spread out creating many alleyways.

We believe this garden to be located here to grow food for the community, educate the community on different water collection methods and plant types they can easily grow at home. We think this location was chosen because of its proximity to the well-known Kauffman Performing Arts Center and it’s on a major road that’s gets a lot of vehicle and foot traffic.

Art Alley

“I hid a, hid a chair over there in that corner there yesterday, someone must’ve stole it…”

-Local homeless man (inaudible)

 

1

Located in a very old and worn down area, extremely poor and patchy surface conditions in the alley, with many weeds and overgrown areas, littered with trash, but still such a beautiful part of the city. The alley is very skinny and is the back door route for many businesses such as a distillery, an art studio, a gym, and others. Almost all the surfaces of the buildings facing this alley are completely covered with streets art.

We believe that this was never intended to be a popular publicly-known location. Due to its hidden nature, alleys such as this are prime locations for graffiti writers and street artists to have free uninterrupted expression. Over time the underground art community recognized this alley for its variety of content and style, and so more and more attention was brought to it, bringing more, artists, then more art, and all of the sudden you have a unique source of culture.

Public Bike Stations

Near a bus stop on the west side of Main Street on the outer edge of the Old Westport district. The surrounding environment consists of large mass buildings spanning full blocks with multiple storefront businesses within them. Main street is very wide in this part of the city and it’s sidewalks are very wide as well. It is a 3 way intersection with Westport Road ending into main.

We believe this to be located here because it is a very commercial area with many places to shop and eat. Westport has a very large amount of foot traffic and this station serves to connect this major part of the city with the other parts via these bike stations.

The Metal Man

Located in an old area with moderate traffic on the North-East corner of the intersection, This guy has been here for a very long time. There is the grinders event space one block south, with Grinders Pizza and bar down the street. On the South- East corner of the intersection is the International Tap House, and on the north side of the street is the Border Brewing Company.

We believe this was placed here by a local artist that knows the area and people well. This strip of 18th street sees a large amount of vehicle and foot traffic, and the area is well known for the Grinders outdoor event space. This metal man is an expression of the rough yet artistic vibe of the area.

Public Podium/Sculpture

Located in a neighborhood park with a community garden to the East, a public library to the South-East and a community center to the South. Huge Oak tree canopies cover the quiet neighborhood full of old houses. The sculpture is inspired by Mayan pyramids and contains drawings by local children on tiles on the faces of the sculpture.

As the focal point to the terraced hill to it’s West, this functions as both a sculpture and public forum. We believe it was put her to celebrate and involve the community.
